Linguini

Description

Linguini is a type of pasta that originated in Italy, commonly referred to as "linguine" or "linguini fini". It is a long, thin and flat noodle, similar to spaghetti but wider in shape. The name "linguini" comes from the Italian word "lingua" which means "tongue", referencing the noodle's shape. Linguini is made from durum wheat semolina and water, and is typically served with seafood or tomato-based sauces.

The texture of linguini is unique, as it is firm and chewy, yet smooth on the surface. Its shape makes it perfect for clinging onto sauces and ingredients, making each bite a perfect blend of pasta and flavor. Linguini dishes are a staple in Italian cuisine and are loved around the world for their versatility and simplicity. It can be served as a main course, side dish, or even as a salad. No matter how it is prepared, linguini is a delicious and satisfying addition to any meal.

Ingredients

  • Linguini Pasta
  • Olive Oil
  • Garlic
  • Red Pepper
  • Chicken
  • Parsley
  • Salt and pepper

Steps

  1. Bring a large pot of salted water to a boil. Add the linguini and cook until al dente, about 8-10 minutes.
  2. While the pasta is cooking, heat the olive oil in a large skillet over medium heat. Add the garlic and red pepper flakes and cook until fragrant, about 1-2 minutes.
  3. Add the cooked linguini to the skillet with the garlic and red pepper flakes. Toss to coat the pasta in the oil and spices.
  4. Add the parsley and basil to the skillet and toss to combine. Cook for 1-2 minutes, until the herbs are slightly wilted.
  5. Remove the skillet from the heat and add the Parmesan cheese. Toss to combine.
  6. Season the linguini with salt and pepper to taste.
  7. Serve hot, garnished with extra Parmesan cheese and chopped herbs if desired.
